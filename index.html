01 · Problem

Why mobility in Misk City needs a unified brain.

Peak-hour crowds, fixed-route shuttles, and disconnected data break the seamless experience the city promises. MMIS tackles four critical friction points:

Difficult wayfinding

Visitors struggle to navigate multi-layer campuses and indoor/outdoor transitions, leading to late arrivals and frustrated guests.

Static traffic control

Non-predictive systems react too late to congestion, forcing shuttles into bottlenecks and increasing emissions.

Unpredictable surges

Events and prayer times create sudden crowd waves that compromise safety unless managed proactively.

Disconnected modes

Bikes, walking paths, logistics fleets, and shuttles operate in silos without shared situational awareness.

03 · How it Works

Three-layer intelligence powering the city.

Sense

Edge sensors, CCTV, ticketing data, micromobility docks, and weather feeds funnel data into the MMIS data plane.

Predict

ML models forecast flows fifteen minutes ahead, evaluate risk scenarios, and expose APIs for partner systems.

Orchestrate

Decision engines push commands to shuttles, logistics fleets, signage, and citizen apps via low-latency control buses.
